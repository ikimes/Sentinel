## Purpose

Identify what Sentinel should copy from the strongest adjacent patterns, product shapes, and market-entry lessons in `current/v1/`.

The goal is not imitation for its own sake.

The goal is to isolate the behaviors that make Sentinel more buyable, more productized, and more defensible without collapsing its own identity.

## Pass 1: Wide-Range Initial Pass

The broad candidate set from `v1` is:

### 1. Copy the narrow entry wedge behavior

The strongest analogs did not enter through a giant new category.

They entered through one painful and recognizable operational thread.

Sentinel should copy that discipline.

### 2. Copy evidence-grade operational trust

Vanta, Drata, ServiceNow, and Veeva-adjacent products all reinforce that evidence, ownership, and readiness are not side features.

They are part of the buying case.

Sentinel should copy the assumption that provenance and reconstruction are table stakes.

### 3. Copy the control-surface packaging

Lower-appetite buyers may buy a control surface before they buy a broader orchestration layer.

Sentinel should copy the packaging logic of landing as a smaller, useful surface before asking buyers to believe in the full company ambition.

### 4. Copy the matter or object-centered model

Palantir-like and OneTrust-like patterns validate that cross-system work becomes more valuable when the product gives users one stable object or matter to work from.

Sentinel should copy the commitment to a shared matter abstraction.

### 5. Copy fast time-to-value and minimal footprint

The analog set repeatedly rewards products that become useful with limited rollout, not enterprise-wide reinvention.

Sentinel should copy the discipline of proving value with one source system, one downstream target, and a small user group.

### 6. Copy operator-centered workflow, not reporting-centered workflow

The stronger product analogs do not stop at dashboards.

They give users a place to act, assign, attest, or resolve.

Sentinel should copy that action orientation.

### 7. Copy coexistence with incumbent systems

The strongest practical analogs respect existing systems of record and do not require buyers to repudiate prior platform choices.

Sentinel should copy that coexistence behavior explicitly.

### 8. Copy context compression only when grounded in provenance

PagerDuty-style narrative and Sentinel's own UI work suggest that readable context is valuable.

Sentinel should copy the pattern of compressing operational complexity into human-usable context only when source evidence remains inspectable.

### 9. Copy expansion by repeatable adjacent workflow, not by abstract platform ambition

The analog set supports land-and-expand only when the next workflow reuses the same center of gravity.

Sentinel should copy expansion by repeated matter pattern, not by broadening language first.

### 10. Copy scenario-backed proof, not slideware proof

The strongest `v1` validation logic came from the minimal-integration proof, the hypothetical partner tests, and the scenario harness idea.

Sentinel should copy the discipline of proving value through one live recurring workflow instead of claiming future generality.

## Pass 2: Narrow-Hardening Pass

Using the `avoid` and `uniquely-prove` pass-1 logic, several broad candidates narrow out quickly.

Sentinel should not try to copy every attractive behavior from the analog set.

The stronger shortlist is:

### 1. Copy narrow, painful workflow entry

This survives because `avoid` pass-1 makes broad category entry a trap, and `uniquely-prove` pass-1 says recurrence and pain density must be earned.

The copied behavior is not merely "start small."

It is "start where the pain is concrete enough to concentrate buyer attention."

### 2. Copy evidence-grade trust as a product requirement

This survives because `avoid` pass-1 eliminates dashboard-only and AI-first positioning, and `uniquely-prove` pass-1 says Sentinel must earn daily-use trust, not just visual clarity.

This means evidence, provenance, and reconstructability are copied as part of the product core, not as compliance garnish.

### 3. Copy minimal-footprint time to value

This survives because the weak hypothetical partner shows that light pain cannot support a large implementation ask, and `uniquely-prove` pass-1 says Sentinel must show value before many integrations.

### 4. Copy operator workspace design, not generic workflow tooling

This survives because `avoid` pass-1 rules out ticketing drift and graph spectacle, and `uniquely-prove` pass-1 says the workspace must answer owner, clock, blocker, evidence, and downstream-state questions better than incumbent combinations.

### 5. Copy respectful overlay behavior with incumbents

This survives because the whole `v1` wedge depends on Sentinel staying beside the systems of record rather than trying to become them.

### 6. Copy land-and-expand packaging through an exception or control surface

This remains valuable, but as a commercial wrapper rather than as the product doctrine itself.

This is the one area where a near-new answer becomes clearer in `v2`:

Sentinel should copy the *commercial entry behavior* of smaller control-surface products without copying their tendency to become only readiness tooling.

## Pass 3: Final Narrow-Hardening Pass

The final set of copied behaviors should now be expressed as strategic rules.

### Rule 1: Copy entry through one painful, regulated follow-up workflow

Why it survives:

- It is the strongest repeated lesson from the analog set.
- It aligns with the strong hypothetical partner and rejects the weak one.

Evidence:

- `analog-market-entry-and-lessons.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment-weaker.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- broad category entry with no concentrated buying motion

### Rule 2: Copy evidence-grade trust as table stakes, not differentiation theater

Why it survives:

- Sentinel loses its authority if it becomes only workflow, only dashboards, or only AI summaries.
- The doctrine and build-vs-buy work both require provenance to be part of the core.

Evidence:

- `product-doctrine-options.md`
- `build-vs-buy-teardown.md`
- `competitive-positioning-comparison.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- becoming a nicer but less accountable coordination layer

### Rule 3: Copy minimal-footprint time to operational value

Why it survives:

- Sentinel must win before buyers commit to large integration programs.
- The first proof has to feel productized, not programmatic.

Evidence:

- `minimal-integration-proof.md`
- `v1-discovery-wrap-and-strategic-thesis.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- becoming an implementation-heavy overlay that dies before proving value

### Rule 4: Copy operator-first workflow surfaces, not reporting-first or graph-first surfaces

Why it survives:

- The workspace became one of the strongest identity improvements in `v1`.
- The comparison work shows dashboards, graph novelty, and generic workflow all weaken distinctness.

Evidence:

- `competitive-positioning-comparison.md`
- `product-doctrine-options.md`
- `ui-low-fidelity-wireframes.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- looking like analytics, generic workflow, or a visualization demo

### Rule 5: Copy coexistence with incumbents and expansion by repeated matter pattern

Why it survives:

- The overlay boundary is part of what makes Sentinel believable.
- Expansion only makes sense if the same matter-centered model survives.

Evidence:

- `v1-discovery-wrap-and-strategic-thesis.md`
- `competitive-positioning-comparison.md`
- `build-vs-buy-teardown.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- suite-replacement drift and undisciplined platform ambition

### Rule 6: Copy the commercial entry pattern of a control surface without reducing the product to readiness tooling

Why it survives:

- Lower-appetite accounts may only buy a thinner first offer.
- Sentinel still needs the deeper workspace and coordination-state identity underneath.

Evidence:

- `product-doctrine-options.md`
- `analog-market-entry-and-lessons.md`
- `beliefs-doubts-and-must-prove-next.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- either overselling the whole company too early or underselling Sentinel into a shallow audit tool

## Locked Conclusions

- Sentinel should copy buying-motion discipline and product-shape discipline more than category language.
- The strongest copied behaviors are narrow entry, evidence-grade trust, operator workspace design, overlay coexistence, and minimal-footprint proof.
- The copied control-surface pattern is valuable only as packaging unless it still points back to the deeper matter-workspace doctrine.

## Still Open

- whether the first commercial wrapper should emphasize exception control, inspection readiness, or matter follow-up equally
- which copied behaviors belong in the first customer story versus the first product build
