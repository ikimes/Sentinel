## Purpose

Identify the few claims that Sentinel must uniquely prove because adjacent products, analogs, and market patterns cannot prove them on Sentinel's behalf.

This is the axis that turns `v2` from strategic polish into strategic accountability.

## Pass 1: Wide-Range Initial Pass

The broad candidate proof set from `v1` is:

### 1. Sentinel must prove a real economic buyer exists

The analogs prove categories can be bought.

They do not prove that Sentinel's first workflow has a durable budget owner.

### 2. Sentinel must prove the chosen matter type is recurrent and painful enough

The current matter is strategically clean.

It is not yet commercially earned.

### 3. Sentinel must prove buyers want an overlay rather than replacement or reporting

This is specific to Sentinel's boundary.

No analog can prove that for it.

### 4. Sentinel must prove the core matter model is stable across repeated examples

The doctrine and system design suggest this.

Only repeated use will prove it.

### 5. Sentinel must prove it creates value before many integrations

This is one of the sharpest commercial requirements from `v1`.

### 6. Sentinel must prove the operator workspace is materially better than internal build combinations

A nice UI is not enough.

The workspace must make work faster, clearer, and more accountable.

### 7. Sentinel must prove provenance-backed context compression is daily useful, not just impressive

The context-window idea is promising.

It still needs to show that users trust and repeatedly use it.

### 8. Sentinel must prove its differentiation survives without graph novelty and without AI novelty

If the product only feels unique with those layers turned on, the underlying wedge is weak.

### 9. Sentinel must prove lower-appetite packaging still points to a larger reusable product core

The exception-control-surface entry path is promising.

It still needs to prove that it is a real on-ramp, not a different business.

## Pass 2: Narrow-Hardening Pass

Using the `copy` and `avoid` pass-1 outputs, several candidate proofs collapse into a tighter set.

Some proof needs are important, but not truly unique.

For example, "users like good context compression" is valuable, but it is not the deepest thing Sentinel alone must prove.

The stronger proof shortlist is:

### 1. Prove that one recurring cross-system follow-up matter is painful enough to create buying behavior

This remains central because without it the entire wedge weakens.

### 2. Prove that a real overlay buying path exists

This combines economic buyer clarity with the overlay-versus-replacement question.

It is more precise than proving "a buyer exists" in the abstract.

### 3. Prove that Sentinel's stable matter model beats custom build drift

This is where the workspace, evidence layer, and coordination-state model all meet.

### 4. Prove that the product wins with minimal footprint

This remains essential because `avoid` pass-1 eliminates implementation-heavy proofs.

### 5. Prove that the workspace answers the core operational questions better than incumbent combinations

This survives because distinctness depends on the daily-use surface, not only on backend structure.

### 6. Prove that the lower-appetite commercial wrapper still leads into the same deeper product

This is the clearest materially new emphasis in `v2`.

`v1` established the possibility of selling a thinner control surface first.

`v2` makes explicit that Sentinel must prove that this wrapper is not a separate business from the deeper matter-workspace doctrine.

## Pass 3: Final Narrow-Hardening Pass

The final unique proof set should be treated as Sentinel's earned-credibility checklist.

### Rule 1: Sentinel must uniquely prove that its first matter type creates real buying pressure, not only conceptual agreement

Why it survives:

- Need is not demand.
- This is the central unresolved commercial question from `v1`.

Evidence:

- `beliefs-doubts-and-must-prove-next.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment-weaker.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- building around a workflow that sounds right but never concentrates enough urgency to buy

### Rule 2: Sentinel must uniquely prove that a real overlay buying path exists

Why it survives:

- The operator sponsor is visible, but the economic buyer is not yet earned.
- The product only works if buyers want a thin coordination layer rather than replacement or reporting alone.

Evidence:

- `beliefs-doubts-and-must-prove-next.md`
- `economic-buyer-map.md`
- `build-vs-buy-teardown.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- having a product people admire but nobody can actually purchase

### Rule 3: Sentinel must uniquely prove that its matter model and workspace outperform internal build on coherence and repeatability

Why it survives:

- Internal build is the most dangerous substitute.
- Sentinel's strongest buy argument is that it productizes what buyers would otherwise rebuild imperfectly.

Evidence:

- `build-vs-buy-teardown.md`
- `competitive-positioning-comparison.md`
- `product-doctrine-options.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- losing to "good enough" custom workflow and reporting stacks

### Rule 4: Sentinel must uniquely prove that it can win with one source system, one downstream target, and a small user group

Why it survives:

- This is the minimum credible path to product proof without requiring a major program.

Evidence:

- `minimal-integration-proof.md`
- `hypothetical-design-partner-assessment.md`
- `v1-discovery-wrap-and-strategic-thesis.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- hiding weak product value behind large implementation scope

### Rule 5: Sentinel must uniquely prove that the workspace answers owner, clock, blocker, downstream-state, and decision-basis questions better than incumbent combinations

Why it survives:

- This is where the product becomes more than backend plumbing.
- It is also where the UI, evidence model, and coordination-state model become one practical answer.

Evidence:

- `competitive-positioning-comparison.md`
- `ui-low-fidelity-wireframes.md`
- `minimal-integration-proof.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- being a clever architecture with no strong daily-use advantage

### Rule 6: Sentinel must uniquely prove that a thinner control-surface entry still compounds into the same deeper product

Why it survives:

- A smaller entry offer may help the buying motion.
- But if it points to a different product center, it weakens the doctrine instead of helping it.

Evidence:

- `product-doctrine-options.md`
- `analog-market-entry-and-lessons.md`
- `v1-discovery-wrap-and-strategic-thesis.md`

Failure mode it prevents:

- splitting Sentinel into one thing that sells and another thing that the long-term vision actually needs

## Locked Conclusions

- Sentinel's unique proof burden is mainly commercial and product-shape oriented, not category-legitimacy oriented.
- The two hardest unique proofs are buying path and repeatable product superiority over internal build.
- Minimal-footprint proof and daily-use superiority are not optional proof steps. They are central.

## Still Open

- whether proof of the first matter type should begin with one cross-market workflow or a simpler single-market analogue
- whether the first thin commercial wrapper should be framed around exception control, inspection readiness, or post-intake follow-up directly
